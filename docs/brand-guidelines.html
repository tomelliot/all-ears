All Ears

Records and transcribes meetings, calls, and audio — a lightweight, privacy-forward macOS app that lives in the menu bar. The name plays on “I’m all ears”: attentive, active listening.

01

Brand Overview

Mission. Capture every conversation faithfully, on the user’s own machine, so nothing worth remembering is lost.

Vision. Listening software that earns trust by staying quiet, local, and out of the way.

Values

Attentive

Present and accurate. The product listens closely so the user doesn’t have to take notes.

Private by default

Local-first. Data belongs to the user; the brand never trades on surveillance.

Quietly premium

Restrained, native, well-made. Craft over noise — a tool, not a platform.

Personality

Attentive · Understated · Warm · Precise · Native. All Ears is the calm colleague who caught every word — not a chatty assistant, not a corporate SaaS.

Audience

Primary: founders, consultants, researchers, and engineers who live in back-to-back calls and value privacy. Secondary: journalists and knowledge workers who record interviews and need reliable, local transcripts.

The Mark & Logo

The mark is a single warm stroke — a cupped ear that doubles as a quotation mark, with an accent dot for the earlobe. Captured speech, made quiet. It is drawn once and used everywhere; do not redraw or substitute it.

Primary lockup

All·Ears

Horizontal lockup — the default. Mark height equals the cap height of the wordmark; the gap between them equals one mark-width.

Two colours maximum: ink (or paper, reversed) plus one accent. The accent lives only in the earlobe dot. When one colour is required — engraving, favicon, template menu-bar icon — use the monochrome version, where the dot becomes a solid ink bulb.

03

Clear Space & Sizing

Give the mark room. Minimum clear space on all sides equals the diameter of the earlobe dot (d). Keep this margin free of type, edges, and other marks.

Minimum sizes

Icon / app
24px min · UI
18px · menu bar
16px · floor

Digital floor is 16px using the monochrome version. Below that, detail collapses — don’t use the mark; use the wordmark or a single accent dot. The full-colour mark should not appear below 24px.

Usage. One accent per surface. Amber is the default; terracotta and coral are seasonal / campaign alternates; ink-navy is the restrained alternate for formal contexts. Never mix two accents in one composition.

Accessibility

Ink on paper clears WCAG AA comfortably (≈15:1). Amber is a graphic accent, not a text colour — don’t set body copy in amber on paper. For accent text, darken to ≥4.5:1 or reserve amber for fills and marks.

Logo Don’ts

The mark is a fixed drawing. Protect its proportions and its single-stroke clarity.

✓Correct — single warm stroke, amber earlobe, upright.
✕Don’t stretch, condense, or distort proportions.
✕Don’t rotate or flip beyond the approved variants.
✕Don’t add shadows, glows, gradients, or bevels.
✕Don’t recolour to AI-blue or any off-brand hue.

08

Voice & Tone

Write the way the product listens: closely, plainly, without performing. State things once. Never oversell privacy — demonstrate it.

We are

Attentivenot eager
Plainnot dumbed-down
Calmnot cold
Precisenot jargony

Principles

Lead with the noun; assert claims as facts.
Short sentences. Active voice.
Say “on your Mac”, not “powered by AI”.
Respect the user’s attention — no urgency tricks.

Tone by context

ContextToneExample
MarketingConfident, quiet“Every meeting, transcribed. Nothing leaves your Mac.”
OnboardingReassuring“Recording stays local. You control what’s kept.”
ErrorsPlain, helpful“Couldn’t reach the microphone. Check Sound settings.”
Recording stateMinimal“Listening…”

The name

“All Ears” — two words, both capitalised. The wordmark sets it with an amber mid-dot: All·Ears. In running text, write “All Ears” (no dot). Never “Allears”, “AllEars”, or “all ears” mid-sentence when referring to the product.
